# Decoding the AI hype 

Each and every day you will open Twitter, Instagram, Youtube, LinkedIn, you will see one common line everywhere. "Claude just released a new model and it is insane. Let me tell you why. Open AI just released a new model and it is insane fast and optimized. Let me tell you why. Gemini just released a new model and blah blah blah" the same line but with a massive hook.

You will see these kind of lines nearly everywhere because it is kind of true that AI is evolving day by day and every model somewhere or nearly better than the previous versions. But it's not mean that it is massive or groundbreaking or revolutionary.

## The sugar coated lie
In 2026, many companies are stating that they are using AI for their workloads, to minimize manual work, to write codes nearly up to 50%, 70% or whatever. But the reality is completely different that the general public are never going to know.

You will see that the companies who are mostly claiming that AI is the future, AI is revolution and they don't need entry level jobs or engineers anymore. AI is going to replace them or whatever. Vibe coding is the future, on the other hand, you will also see in their career pages. They often roll out employments like software engineering, software development, frontend, backend or any role which is either for internship or entry level. So the main question is if they are claiming that AI is revolutionary, almost 50 to 70% code is written by AI nowadays, then why they are actively looking for engineers ? Like does that even make sense ?

And most of the time you will see these claims are coming from big tech companies, mostly someone from Google, OpenAI, Anthropic, Microsoft or NVIDIA or any big tech companies which is popular and actively working in AI.

## The hidden truth
There is no doubt that in 2026, the AI tools which are present in the market are far more capable of what they have done in previous years. Mainly the time when Open AI launched ChatGPT in 2022, that time was completely different and within the span of three years, AI has seen a massive revolution. But here is the catch. AI has seen a massive revolution on its own journey, on its own capability. But that doesn't mean that AI is going to replace entry level jobs, or entry level engineers, or the actual ones who understand systems.

In this current scenario, one thing that is rapidly growing more than AI, that is marketing. Whatever kind of marketing you will perform, it will spread across the globe within minutes. Either it is a good marketing or a bad marketing. And mostly. The individuals who are claiming that they are not going to need any entry level jobs or AI is going to take away all the jobs, AI is revolutionary but the reality is they are pretending. Actually they are pretending that AI is going to this or that.

And the reason why these individuals or companies are creating this hype is because they know very well that if do this AI marketing, they will create this hype even if the hype is not real for now or even for one or two years from now still many companies or investors will give them funding, a lot of funding and it can be of millions or billions, and they can use that money to improve AI. Which we don't know that they will improve how much or how much capable it will become in future. But these companies for now just claiming false.

## The hype of vibe coding
Today whatever AI vibe coding tool you use either it is GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Claude or whatever. These tools are just a part of this hype and the sole purpose of creating these tools is to generate more hype. No doubt they work fine for small projects, prototypes and demo projects, but the one thing that they do in a large scale more than coding is accelerate the marketing and AI hype.

People need to understand that whenever these companies or any startup launches a AI product, they test it on a very small environment. Obviously they will not enclose that environment to you or in general public. They will just showcase it that look, this is what our AI model is capable of but everything is already recorded or predefined. So you are just seeing the output, the final output, and you can't just say that yeah, this AI model is revolutionary, which is absolute dumb.

And just by looking at the AI hype and these outputs on predefined or limited environments, many companies think that yeah, the AI hype is getting intense and actually AI is doing stuff and we should invest more in AI to improve AI, to make use of AI in our day-to-day lifestyle. And here is how these big tech companies are generating more fund. Even though the AI is still the same, but showing the output is changing time by time.

***
Now one question may arise in your mind that if AI is not that much advanced or revolutionary till now then why we see news sometimes that AI has advanced in medical or AI has gone away too big in space and satellite imaginary. Or robotic cars and so on. Yeah, you can say that and it is true that at some extent AI is going beyond. While integrating in embedded systems or actual hardwares or in systems where AI is actually helping on leveraging the current tech but here the question of actual delivery and the marketing separates when the claim comes that the AI will consume all the software development jobs or AI will do every coding stuff and software developers will be vanished. Here is the actual marketing and the reality line separates.

## So, should I worry or not ?
Answering this question, you should ask this first to yourself that first of all on which profession you are currently in, second is how your current profession is getting affected by AI like how AI is helping in your current profession and if you think that at any point your work will somehow will get disrupted by AI or AI can do more than what you are doing till now, then the one thing you should need to worry about is adapt with AI. Learn new AI tools and technologies, learn to integrate in in your current setup and work more effectively, not just fear and hope that no AI will not become very much advanced than humans or anything like that.

Right now AI is not that much advanced or revolutionary, it is true, but it does not mean in future this same point will be valid in around two or three years. We don't know the future at all. Nobody knows. So it is better to adapt with AI but to also understand systems well. AI can write code, AI can integrate in your work and it can help you. But in deep down you should know that AI is doing what Because in future if anything goes wrong or any error arises then you will be responsible for figuring it out and fixing it, at that time you can't just put it on any AI extension or tool.
